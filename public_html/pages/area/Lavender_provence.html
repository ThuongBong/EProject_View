History of the Lavender provence

Lavandula angustifolia:(English name: English lavender, common lavender, true lavender, narrow-leaved lavende) is a plant of the genus Lavandula, the Lamiaceae family. Lavender is an annual bush with a strong odor, originating in the Mediterranean. Its scientific name Lavendula, from the Latin lavare, means washing (or bathing). The lavender plant was once known thousands of years ago, from ancient Greece. The Romans brought lavender flowers throughout Europe, all the places they came from, in order to create a local supply of lavender essential oil. During the Middle Ages, it was considered as the herb of love.



Due to the clean aroma and insect repellency, it is a widely used herb. It was used to disinfect wounds during wartime. Vanilla is also a popular plant in the garden. Lavender is also packaged in aromatic pouches to protect the senses (biting clothes) and make up the fragrance for the room, clothing. Also used as a sedative, and also antibacterial. Lavender is a strong antiseptic, healing wounds, burns burns (used in the first and second world war). Lavender is one of the sacred herb in the summer. Flowering period is from June to August.

Common:



The original lavender was the coastal area of the Mediterranean, usually on dry slopes in Dalmatia and Greece, and in the Toscana region of Italy. Lavandula angustifolia usually grows on rocky slopes and dry and sometimes on the edge of the forest.



Lavender is a typical plant of Provence, France, the lavender fields are often large and are tourist attractions. The area of local flower growing has halved in the years 2002-2012, due to pests and some cold periods with snowstorms.

Sort and use:



Lavender has many different types. The most well-known species are Lavandula angustifolia, Hidcote purple and Dwarf Munstead. Some have larger bodies and grow taller up to 1 meter.



As a herb, lavender has many uses. Its fragrance is placed in a closet that can be kept for months. Not only does it work as a cure for migraine. Because it has a tonic effect, lavender is often used as a tea for headaches, depression, sunburn. People often combine the lavender flowers hung up and dried, when the flowers are completely dry, can be used for a long time. Leaves can also do the same but it does not aroma with flowers.
