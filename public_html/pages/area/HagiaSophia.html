History of the Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia was originally an Orthodox Basilica, later the mosque, and is now a museum in Istanbul, Turkey. Especially famous for its large domes, this building is considered the epitome of Byzantine architecture, and is considered to have "changed the history of architecture." This was the largest church in the world in nearly a century. thousand years, until the Cathedral of Seville completed in 1520.



The building was originally built as a church from 532 to 537 on the orders of the Byzantine Emperor Justinian, and was the third Holy Catholic Church to be built at this site. destroyed by rebels). The building was designed by two Isidorus architects Miletus and Anthemius of Tralles. The church has a collection of relics and has a 15-meter-high silver wall. It is the central church of the Eastern Orthodox Church and the headquarters of the Patriarch of Ecumenical Constantinople for nearly 1000 years.
