History of the Perito Moreno Glacier

Perito Moreno Glacier is a perennial ice mass (with a lower density than regular ice), continually moving by its own weight; It forms where snow accumulates and goes beyond the abstraction (eg, melting and sublimation) over many years, usually centuries. Glaciers are slowly deformed and flowing due to the pressure caused by their mass, resulting in crevasses, seracs, and many other prominent forms. They also erode rocks and debris from the ground to create topographic forms such as arches and arches. Glaciers are formed only on the ground and are distinct from the much thinner ice on the sea and lakes that form on the water.

History:



Glaciers are the largest freshwater reservoir on Earth. Many glaciers from temperate, mountainous, and seasonal climates hold ice water throughout the cold season and flush out water. Summer is high because melting glaciers create water that is especially important for plants, animals and humans when other water sources are scarce. In environments such as Antarctica and high altitude, the difference in temperature between seasons is often insufficient to melt ice.

Structure:



Glaciers separate into regions depending on snow dunes on the surface and melting conditions. The erosion zone is the area where a certain block of glaciers disappears. Equilibrium line separates the wear area and accumulative area; That is the height that new snow forms by accumulating equal amounts of ice lost by wear. The upper part of the glacier, where the accumulation passes the erosion, is called the accumulation area. In general, the accumulation area accounts for 60-70% of the glacial surface, much more if glaciers break into icebergs.

Geography:



The permanent snow required for glacial formation is influenced by factors such as soil gradients, snowfall and winds. Glaciers can be found at any latitude except 20 - 27 degrees north to south of the equator where there is a presence of the Hadley Circumcline, reducing the amount of rainfall so much that with the sunshine from The sun, eternal snow lies at over 6500 m (21330 ft). However, between 19 degrees north and 19 degrees south, the rainfall is higher and the mountains over 5000 meters (16,400 ft) usually have eternal snow.

Glacier geography:



As the glaciers flow through the rock, they soften and pull the ice cubes into the ice. This process is called plucking, which is caused by the water of the glacier that penetrates into the cracks in the ice shelves and then freezes and expands. This bloat causes ice to act as a lever that loosens ice. As a result, sediments of all sizes become part of the glacier. If a glacier is disappearing and there are enough rock debris, it can become a glacier, like the Timpanogos glacier in Utah.
