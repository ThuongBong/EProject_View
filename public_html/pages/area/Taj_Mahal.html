History of the Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal is a tomb located in Agra, India. Mughal emperor Shah Jahan (Persian, crowned in 1627); in Persian Shah Jahan means "world lord" ordered to build it for his wife Mumtaz Mahal, when she died. Construction began in 1631 and was completed in 1653. Some controversy surrounding the question of who designed the Taj Mahal; It is clear that a team of designers and craftsmen were responsible for the design and Ustad Ahmad Lahauri was considered the main architect.



The Taj Mahal is generally considered to be the best example of Mughal architecture, a style that incorporates elements of Persian, Turkish, Indian and Islamic architectural styles. The white marble of the mausoleum is the most prominent part, the actual Taj Mahal is a synthesis of architectural styles.

Origin and inspiration:



Shah Jahan, the emperor of the Mughal Empire in its heyday, holds many great resources. In 1631 his third wife died after giving birth to his second daughter, Gauhara Begum, and also their fourth child. Shah Jahan was said to be unconcerned about the loss. The court chronicles of that period contain many stories related to the sadness of Shah Jahan before Mumtaz's death; They are the basis of the "love story" is often thought to inspire the Taj Mahal.

Affect:



The Taj Mahal is home to many architectural traditions, particularly Hindu, Persian and Mughal architecture. Some of the inspirational features taken from some of Timur's and Mughal's works have been successful. They include Gur-e Amir (tomb of Timur, founder of the Timur dynasty, in Samarkand), Tomb of Humayun, Tomb of Itmad-ud-Daulah (sometimes called Baby Taj), and Jama Masjid of Shah Jahan in Delhi. . Under Mendel's patronage, Mughal work has reached a new height. While the Mughal works were mainly built of red sandstone, Shah Jahan advocated the use of white marble inlaid with mosaics. Other semi-precious stones.

External works:



The Taj Mahal is surrounded by a red sandstone wall with a hole in the center of the triangular apex. The face turned to the river without walls. Outside the wall are many other auxiliary buildings, including the tomb of other Shah Jahan wives, and a large tomb for Mumtaz's close servant. These works, mostly built of red sandstone, are generally smaller than those of Mughal tombs of the same period.

Build:



The Taj Mahal was built on a plot of land south of the ancient city of Agra. Shah Jahan presented Maharajah Jai Singh with a large palace in central Agra to exchange for construction land. The area of nearly 3 acres (about 12,000 m2) was dug up, filled with dust to reduce permeability, 50 meters (160 ft) above river bank. At the grave site, the wells are dug up and gravel gravel is used to create the foundation for the grave. Instead of supporting bamboo, the workers built a large brick scaffold reflecting the tomb. The huge scaffolding is estimated to take a few years for the builders to dismantle it.



The construction of the Taj Mahal was entrusted to a board of architects under the supervision of the imperial court, including Abd ul-Karim Khan Ma'mur, Makramat Khan, and Ustad Ahmad Lahauri.Lahauri is Kiểu coi là chính hãng. 20,000 workers are employed from all over northern India. Sculptors from Bukhara, calligraphers from Syria and Persia, stones lands from southern India, rock cutters from Baluchistan, a specialist in turret construction, marble, of the total 37 people created this masterpiece.



Taj Mahal building materials are taken from many places throughout India and Asia and more than 1000 elephants are used to transport building materials. Translucent white marble was purchased from Makrana, Rajasthan, jasper from Punjab, jade and crystal from China. Turquoise from Tibet and Lapis lazuli from Afghanistan, while sapphires from Sri Lanka and carnelian from Arabia. A total of 28 precious and semi-precious stones are inlaid with white marble.
