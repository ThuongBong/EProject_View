About Hoi An Ancient Town
Posted on October 11,2017 by admin
-About Hoi An Ancient Town
Hoi An Bridge

A long-standing world-famous place, recognized by Unesco as a world cultural heritage site, is a popular tourist destination in central Vietnam.

Hoi An is an ancient Vietnamese town, located in the confluence of Thu Bon River in the coastal plain of Quang Nam Province, about 30km south of Danang. Hoi An was known in the international market with many different names such as Lam Ap, Faifo, Hoai Pho and Hoi An.

Being the only traditional South East Asian seaport in Vietnam, Hoi An retains almost intact over a thousand architectural monuments such as streets, houses, conventions, communal houses, pagodas , shrines, churches, ancient wells, ancient tombs ... The architecture has both traditional artistic nuances of Vietnam, and show the cultural integration with Western and Western countries. Over the centuries, customs, rituals, cultural activities, beliefs as well as traditional dishes are preserved and preserved along with generations of the old people. Hoi An also has a natural environment, peaceful with small villages, lovely crafts, such as carpentry, bronze, pottery ...

Researchers believe that ancient architecture in Hoi An has been rebuilt since the early 19th century, although the construction year may be much older. Ancient architecture is most prominent in the Old Quarter. Covered in the area of ​​Minh An Ward, the ancient town has an area of ​​about 2km², most of the famous relics in Hoi An. Streets in the old town are short and narrow, with winding, horizontal lines along the chessboard. The topography of the Old Quarter is gradually tilted from north to south. The buildings in the Old Quarter are built mostly of traditional materials: brick, wood and no more than two floors. Visitors can easily spot the time not only in the architectural style of every project that is everywhere: on the roof of yin-yang tiles covered with moss and grass; gray walls, old; Sculptures of a strange animal or an ancient story.

Hoi An

For many centuries, Hoi An has been a meeting place of many cultures in the world. Besides the customary customs of the Vietnamese people have more custom of the community of foreign residents to settle as stone worship; worshiping Ong Ong fish of coastal Central Coast; Natural phenomena such as rain, wind, thunder, lightning or sacred objects (ancient trees), ...

The Chinese community in Hoi An has the habit of worshiping gods such as Thien Hau, Quan Cong, the Great Bao Dai, Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva. They regularly organize festivals or other religious activities such as Lunar New Year (lunar January), Thanh Minh (lunar March), Doan Ngo Trung Thu (15/8), Trung Cuu (9/9 lunar calendar), Ha Noi (15/10 lunar calendar).

These diverse social and cultural factors make up the community of Hoi An residents.

Hoi An people who are rich in cultural traditions and soon exchange with the outside world, do not know themselves have formed a unique cultural identity and preserved and preserved through generations until today. . The life of the people here in favor of introspection, subtle shade. With the city of Hoi An as a large ancient home in which a large family living together crowded with the townspeople gentle close and hospitable; friendly, friendly hosts; gentle women, skillful, gentle; The children are polite, obedient ... to create a community of peaceful living together peacefully, for generations and so on.

The richness and diversity of Hoi An people's cultural identity is also reflected in traditional dishes such as high-rise, wonton, rice cake, spicy cake ... from many generations. Today, diners still have the opportunity to enjoy. Life has changed over the years, but Hoi An people are not lost the familiar customs, cultural festivals have long ago, all the ancient capital here are cherished ...
