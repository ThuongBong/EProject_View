Taj Mahal, the sparkling and magnificent temple
Posted on October 11,2017 by admin
-Taj Mahal is a tomb located in Agra, India, a tourist attraction that attracts millions of visitors each year.

The architecture is located in the city of Agra state of Uttar Pradesh , 200 km south of New Delhi . Agra was the capital of the Mughal emperors who ruled northern India from the 16th to the 19th centuries. The Mughal dynasty prospered most in the emperors of Akbar, Jehangir , and Shah Jehan .

One of the seven wonders of the world

The Taj Mahal is one of the seven wonders of the world, and some Western historians argue that the beauty of this architecture is something that no other building passes.

Taj Mahal is built entirely of white marble. Beautiful scenery especially at sunrise and sunset. The Taj Mahal seems to be the most brilliant in the moonlight. On a foggy morning, visitors experience the Taj Mahal as if sunk by the beauty of the place when viewed from across the Yamuna River .

Beautiful reflection.

Architectural style and construction process

The Taj Mahal is considered to be the finest model of Mughal architecture , a style that incorporates elements of Persian, Turkish, Indian and Islamic architectural styles. Although the white marble dome of the mausoleum is the most prominent part, the Taj Mahal is actually a collection of architectural styles.

Taj Mahal was listed as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1983 and is described as a "masterpiece that should be admired internationally among world heritage."



Construction of the Taj Mahal began in Agra shortly after the death of Queen Mahal Mumtaz . Construction began in 1632 and the Taj Mahal was built for a period of 16 years, employing 20,000 workers. It was completed in 1648.



Some controversy surrounding the question of who designed the Taj Mahal ? Apparently a team of designers and artisans was responsible for the design and Ustad Ahmad Lahauri was considered the main architect. After finishing, he ordered all the builders' hands to be cut so that they could never build a beautiful temple like this.

The Taj Mahal is built on a 304m wide and 580m long plot. In the center of the tomb is an octagonal basilic floor, each side is 100m long. White stone marble and red sandstone on high.

It is thanks to this special material that the Taj Mahal can change color depending on the intensity of sunlight, each time frame, the tomb has its own color, especially on the moonlight, Taj Mahal like a sparkling jewel.



White round marble tentacles, surrounded by four small round domes. Four corners are four 40 m high tower. The tomb is decorated with many mosaic carpets. There are mosaic-shaped contours of 12 gemstones. The approximate 80m high up in the blue sky as a jewel.

"Zoom" is closer to this.

The highlight is marble floors divided into black and white chess tables, four high towers (about 40 m) at four corners and the magnificent arch in the center. On the way there are archways that carved the Qur'ân lines, impressing visitors. Sophistication and splendor are evident in every pillar of stone, carved out of 50 precious stones per square foot.




And finally the romantic love story

This historical work can be seen as a symbol of love with the story of the immortal king Shah Jahan for his queen Mahal Mumtalz .

Portrait of Emperor Shah Jahan and Queen Mahal Mumtalz.

According to legend, in 1631, Mumtaz Mahal , the wife of emperor Shah Jahan (crowned in 1627) died after giving birth to his fourteenth child.

The graves of the king and queen are placed in a very sacred place.

Compassionate before the death of his beloved wife, the king ordered the construction of a tomb by the river Yamuna. According to Islam, Emperor Shah Jahan has the right to marry four wives, but because of his love for Mumtaz Mahal , he only marries her.

After 19 years, he spent the rest of his life alone, and spent 16 years watching the construction of the Taj Mahal to express his love for his deceased wife.

Looking across the Yamuna River.

The court chronicles of that period contain many stories related to the sadness of Shah Jahan before the death of Mumtaz. The book records that: just one night after his wife died, the beard and hair of the king became white

The image of the swan reminds people of the emperor's emotional love story.
Interior of the church in the Taj Mahal.

Thousands of tourists visit the temple every day. Taj Mahal is becoming more attractive when tourists are told about the love of the Indian emperor for his wife.
