First opened the highest floor of the Roman arena to welcome visitors
Posted on October 11,2017 by admin
-The Colosseum Colosseum, which dates back to Roman times, will be open to visitors from 1 November.

4th and 5th floors of Colosseum (Rome, Italy) will be officially opened to the public from 1/11. In addition, visitors to these floors also pass through a corridor was first opened.


These two floors are located 52 meters above the ground. Guests will enjoy views of the surrounding area, Palatine Hill, a corner of Rome and the ancient Roman square.


In ancient Rome, the 4th and 5th floors were seats reserved for the lower classes in society. This location is located far from the main stage. However, the Italian authorities have restored the two floors to give visitors an amazing experience when coming to Italy.


Tickets to visit the 4th and 5th floors of the Roman arena are 9 Euro (nearly 240,000), visitors must book in advance. Excursions to this floor are only possible when the delegation has 25 people, must accompany the tour guide to ensure safety.


Colosseum Colosseum is undergoing a multi-million dollar restoration funded by the Tod's shoe company. Renovation work, including restoration of dirt on the wall, renovation of the north and south face, replacement of metal doors and fences on the bottom floor.


Colosseum is the top tourist destination in Rome (Italy). The structure is oval structure, accommodating more than 50,000 people. The height of the arena equals a building of more than 10 floors.


This work began under the reign of Emperor Vespasian in 72 AD and was completed in 80 AD under Emperor Titus. Subsequently, under King Domitian (81-96 AD), repairs were continued. All three emperors are of the Flavian dynasty.


In ancient times, the Colosseum was the place where fights between gladiators and wild beasts took place. These events attracted crowds. In the 80 doors of the arena, 76 doors were used for the audience, two for the Emperor and two for the gladiators.


Every year millions of visitors visit the Colosseum. In 1349, an earthquake struck the southern wall of the building. However, it still stands horribly over the rain, becoming one of the most outstanding works of the Roman Empire to this day.
